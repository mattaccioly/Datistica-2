## Slide 2 — The world we're building toward

**Most AI tells you what will happen. Almost none tells you what to do about it.**

We believe the next decade of value won't come from bigger predictions — it will come from turning prediction into the *right decision*, under real constraints, real risk, and real cost.

We're building the lab that owns that layer in Latin America.

*Presenter note: this is the thesis in three sentences. Everything after substantiates it.*

## Slide 3 — The problem

**Prediction got cheap. Good decisions didn't.**

- Any team can now forecast, score, or classify. The forecast is no longer the bottleneck — **the decision that follows it is.**
- That's where value leaks: a strong model produces an output, and a human guesses the decision under constraints, cost, and uncertainty the model never saw.
- In Latin America the gap is worse: high-stakes capital and operational decisions are still made on **informal, non-data-driven heuristics**, exposed to bias — validated in our own field interviews and the Kauffman literature. [18]
- Meanwhile the region's deep technical talent is **underused or leaving** — chronic PhD underemployment and brain drain. [18]

*Presenter note: name a concrete decision in the room's sector (e.g., "which wells to recompletion-invest this quarter"). Make the gap visceral.*

## Slide 4 — Why now

- **The playbook is proven.** The frontier's best new labs are researcher-to-founder spinouts that lead with science and bind it to product — Sakana (~US$2.65B valuation, research-first, sovereign-market path) [10], Black Forest Labs (Stable-Diffusion creators → focused lab → enterprise) [11], Periodic Labs (US$300M seed, "AI scientists") [12], Humans& (US$480M seed, "tightly integrate science and product") [13]. **Never executed in LATAM as an independent lab.**
- **Public capital is flowing.** Brazil's national AI plan (PBIA 2024–2028) commits ~**R$23bn** [7]; in 2025, **39% of startup capital raised in Brazil** went to AI-intensive companies [6]; FINEP + BNDES have launched a dedicated AI investment fund [6].
- **The market is inflecting.** LATAM AI ~**US$4.7bn (2024) → ~US$30bn by 2033 (~23% CAGR)**, with Brazil the largest market (~25% share) [3]; Microsoft alone committed **US$2.7bn** to Brazilian cloud/AI infrastructure [3].
- **The capability is ready.** Decision-focused ML and optimization have matured from theory to deployable methods.

*Presenter note: "Right layer, right region, right moment, and the money is already moving."*

## Slide 5 — Our solution

**Datistica builds the decision layer: predict → integrate → optimize.**

One scientific spine, not a grab-bag:

1. **Predict** — forecasts and risk models built for *calibration and robustness*, not leaderboard scores.
2. **Integrate** — fuse many disagreeing models (ML, statistical, structural) into one coherent, trustworthy picture. *(Our signature — the keystone.)*
3. **Optimize / Decide** — turn that picture into the decision that's actually optimal under your constraints, cost, and risk appetite.

We don't sell a model. We sell **judgment you can deploy.**

*Presenter note: the large models are the brain; we build the judgment. Say it once, clearly.*

## Slide 8 — Business model

**Three tiers, climbing from cash-now to scale-later:**

1. **Lab (bespoke R&D)** — high-touch, research-grade decision systems for a few flagship clients with hard problems and proprietary data. *Funds the science; generates case studies.*
2. **Service-as-a-software** — sector decision engines, refined per client, served via API. *The repeatable middle.*
3. **Products** — productized decision tools / open-core with commercial licensing. *The scalable layer.*

**Capital stack, sequenced (Sakana-style):** non-dilutive grants → flagship Lab revenue → recurring Service revenue → VC once a product shows pull. Target strategics who can both **fund and buy.** [10]

*Presenter note: emphasize we reach revenue early and don't depend on a single funding source.*

## Slide 9 — Landscape

**Everyone local is building the brain. The judgment layer is empty.**

| | Foundation-model layer | **Decision layer** |
|---|---|---|
| **LATAM** | Maritaca (PT-BR LLM) [14], WideLabs (sovereign LLM/PaaS) [15], Dharma-AI (specialized SLMs) [16] | **Datistica — open** |
| **Global** | OpenAI, Anthropic, etc. | Niche / fragmented |

- Local players compete on **models** (an arms race we'd lose on compute against players with 100× the capital).
- We compete on the **decision** that sits on top of any model — where the data is proprietary and frontier-scale compute isn't required.
- Closest structural analog: **Dharma-AI** (Rio, research-led, productizing) — but their bet is model *efficiency*; ours is *decision quality*. [16]
- Regional research strength exists (Chile leads the LATAM AI index; Brazil holds >90% of regional high-performance compute) [17] — but no independent, research-first lab owns this layer.

*Presenter note: the empty cell is the pitch. Point at it.*

## Slide 10 — Why we win (moat)

- **We optimize the decision, not the score.** Most AI is graded on accuracy; we're graded on whether the decision was right — a harder, more useful, more defensible target.
- **Integration is the keystone.** Fusing heterogeneous models under real constraints is genuinely hard and under-tooled — and it's our signature.
- **Proprietary data + proprietary decisions.** Every Lab engagement generates data and decision feedback that exist nowhere else and compound into the next system.
- **A talent magnet no competitor has built** (next slide).
- **First mover in an empty layer** in a region with real data, real pain, and a public-capital tailwind.

*Presenter note: lead with "we optimize the decision, not the score" — it's the line they'll remember.*

## Slide 11 — The talent engine (our unfair advantage)

**Latin America over-produces PhDs its academy and industry can't absorb — and loses them.** [18]

University research means **rigid funding** and **heavy IP haircuts** for the researcher. We offer the opposite:

- **Publish freely** — your record grows with us.
- **Own your work** — real IP participation, legal under the Marco Legal de CT&I (Lei 13.243/2016). [19]
- **Own a research line**, not a ticket queue.
- **Stay a scientist** — keep academic affiliation, collaborations, mobility.
- **Be an owner** — founding researchers share the upside.

**The pitch:** *Do frontier science, keep your name on it, own a piece of what you build, and stay a scientist.*

*Presenter note: this is the slide that makes investors believe you can recruit — your scarcest input.*

## Slide 12 — Roadmap

- **Phase 0 — Foundations (now → ~3 mo):** founder agreement; the 3 research faces; 1–2 papers from existing work; free accelerators (NVIDIA Inception, Google for Startups, AWS, MS for Startups).
- **Phase 1 — Reputation + first revenue (~3–9 mo):** one paid Lab pilot on proprietary data; keep shipping papers.
- **Phase 2 — Public funding (~6–18 mo):** FINEP/FNDCT, FAPERJ, EMBRAPII (via PUC-Rio), PBIA/NIB calls. [7][8][9]
- **Phase 3 — Scale R&D + find the product (~12–24 mo):** hire researchers; let deployments reveal the product.
- **Phase 4 — VC (~24 mo+):** raise on papers + deployments + a recruiting story no one in LATAM can match.

**Beachhead split:** flagship research *demo* = VC capital-allocation (our data, ready now); first paying *sectors* = energy/O&G and healthcare operations.

*Presenter note: stress non-dilutive first, dilutive later — capital efficiency is the story.*
