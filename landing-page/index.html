The gap

Everyone can predict. Almost no one decides well.

The last decade made prediction cheap. Any team can now forecast demand, score risk, or flag an anomaly. Yet the hardest part of any operation was never the forecast — it's the decision that comes after it: what to do, under real constraints, real costs, and real uncertainty, when being wrong is expensive.

That gap — between a model's output and a defensible decision — is where value leaks out of most AI projects. It's the gap we close.

What we do

From signal to decision.

We work across three connected layers. They're not three products — they're one pipeline.

01

Predict

See what's coming, honestly.

Forecasts, risk, and time-to-event models built for calibration and robustness — not leaderboard scores. The number means something, and it holds up when the world shifts.

02

Integrate

Make your models agree.

Real organizations have many models that disagree. We fuse them — machine learning, statistical, and structural — into one coherent, trustworthy picture. This is our signature work, and it's harder than it sounds.

03

Decide

Turn it into the best action.

We translate predictions into the decision that's actually optimal under your constraints, your risk appetite, and your cost of being wrong. This is where prediction finally pays for itself.

How we're different

We're not racing to build the biggest model.

The world has enough giant general-purpose models, and more capital chasing them than we could ever match. We chose the layer they ignore: the decision layer.

Think of it this way — the large models are the brain. We build the judgment: the system that takes any model's prediction and turns it into the best possible decision. That's where industrial decisions are actually made, where the data is yours and no one else's, and where world-class work doesn't require a billion-dollar compute bill.

We optimize the decision, not the score.

Most AI is graded on accuracy. We grade ourselves on whether the decision was right — a different, harder, and far more useful target. Models that win benchmarks routinely lose money. Models built around the decision don't.

Built to be deployed.

Every line of research we pursue has to clear one bar: it must be both publishable as science and deployable as a product. If a result can't improve a real decision, it isn't ours.
